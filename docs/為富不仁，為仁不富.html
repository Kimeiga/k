为富不仁，为仁不富

wei2 fu4 bu4 ren2 , wei2 ren2 bu4 fu4

Definitions: the benevolent man cannot be rich and vice versa (idiom, from Mencius). It is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for a rich man to enter the kingdom of heaven (Matthew 19:24).
